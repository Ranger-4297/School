Logic Operators

What are Logic Operators?

A logic operation is a symbol or word that joins two or more information phrases. It's most commonly used to see if a relationship between two phrases is true or false.

Examples of logic operators:

AND

Only when both of its inputs are true would the AND logic operator return true. The output is false if either of the inputs is false.
The AND operation is generally expressed as && in computer programming.
The AND operation of two inputs A and B can be expressed as AB in Boolean algebra.
